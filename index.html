Go-To-Market & Compliance

Strategy for penetrating the US market, starting with the high-density Eastern Seaboard corridor.

Phased Rollout: I-95 Corridor

Phase 1: Tri-State Area (NY/NJ/PA)

Highest dealer density. Focus on independent dealers who lack enterprise tools. Regulatory Focus: NY "Lemon Laws" and stringent disclosure requirements.

Phase 2: The South (FL/GA/NC)

High volume of lease returns and fleet vehicles. Partner with regional auction houses. Regulatory Focus: Flood damage disclosures (Hurricane risks).

Phase 3: Integration & Scale

Full API integration with major DMS (Dealer Management Systems) like Reynolds & Reynolds or CDK Global.

Long-Term Plays: Solar & Hydrogen

As the industry fragments into BEV (Battery), FCEV (Hydrogen), and Solar-Assisted vehicles, inspection complexity increases.

Solar Roof Efficiency

Micro-cracks in solar skins (like on Fisker Ocean or Aptera) drastically reduce range. Visual AI is needed to detect these specific defects.

Hydrogen Tanks

High-pressure tank integrity checks will move from industrial settings to consumer inspections. Safety compliance is the #1 value prop here.

Grid Integration

V2G (Vehicle to Grid) capability verification. Does the car actually feed back to the grid as promised? A new metric for value.

The 5 Blind Spots

Additional critical research factors often overlooked in standard feasibility studies.

1. The "Repo" Tsunami

If the US economy softens, repossession volumes spike. Banks need quick, cheap, trustworthy condition reports to liquidate assets at auction. Can Carmyn scale specifically for the Repossession Recovery industry?

2. OBD-III Data Access Wars

OEMs are locking down the OBD port data. If Carmyn relies on plugging into the car for data, they are at the mercy of GM/Ford/Tesla allowing access. They need a "Right to Repair" strategy.

3. The Technician Shortage

Even digital tools need a human operator. The US has a massive shortage of automotive techs. Carmyn's UI must be simple enough for a gig-worker or lot attendant, not just a master mechanic.

4. Fraud Sophistication

Digital odometers can be rolled back; software can mask battery issues. Carmyn needs "Digital Forensics" capabilities, not just visual inspection.

5. Ride-Share Fleet Cycles

Uber/Lyft cars age in "dog years." A dedicated inspection module for high-mileage interior wear (vomit, tears, smoke) specifically for TNC (Transportation Network Company) fleets could be a niche entry point.
